Email Security Tips to Protect Your Inbox

Email remains one of the most common targets for cyberattacks. From phishing scams to data leaks, your inbox is constantly at risk. Following a few smart practices can help you stay protected.

🔐 1. Use Strong, Unique Passwords

Avoid using the same password across multiple sites. A strong password should include a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Password managers can help you generate and store secure passwords.

📩 2. Avoid Suspicious Emails

Never click on unknown links or download attachments from unfamiliar senders. Phishing emails often look legitimate but are designed to steal your data.

AD SPACE

🛡️ 3. Use Temporary Emails

When signing up for unknown websites, use a disposable email instead of your real one. This prevents spam and protects your primary inbox.

📱 4. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Two-factor authentication adds an extra layer of security by requiring a second verification step. Even if your password is compromised, your account remains protected.

🚫 5. Don’t Share Your Email Publicly

Posting your email on forums or websites makes it easy for spammers and bots to collect it. Always keep your primary email private.
